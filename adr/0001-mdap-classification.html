ADR-0001: Classify SKY365 as an MDAP

Accepted with qualifications · 2026-07-17

Context

SKY365 contains business applications that may resemble ERP modules, but its architectural identity must be decided from executable mechanisms. Source shows module-scoped Controller catalogs and JSON schemas being resolved into runtime schema objects that drive multiple behaviors.

Decision

Document SKY365 as a Metadata-Driven Application Platform. Treat Controllers and their schemas/catalogs as the central application metadata family. Do not describe the platform itself as an ERP product.

Evidence

  • DynamicScreenSchema models fields, detail schemas, sections, views, rules, actions, lookups and many-to-many relationships.
  • DynamicControllerCatalogService discovers, merges, normalizes and deduplicates modular Controller metadata.
  • Sky365DynamicRuntimeService resolves/loads schemas and performs CRUD, lookup, authorization and dynamic action behavior from them.
  • MetadataGraphService ingests and links systems, modules, controllers, schemas, workflows, dashboards and reports.
  • DynamicToolGateway exposes catalog/runtime capabilities to AI/tool consumers.

Qualifications and consequences

  • “Central” does not mean “the only metadata.” Workflow, dashboard and report definitions may remain linked domains.
  • Engine names require executable evidence; planned names are not cataloged as implemented.
  • All documentation must preserve source revision and distinguish verified, partial and not-found claims.
  • Future Semantic, Agent, Vector or Compiler engines should derive stable identities and authorization from the existing metadata model rather than create a disconnected model.

Alternatives rejected

ERP-first classification: rejected because it describes a possible application domain, not the platform mechanism. Claim that one Controller controls everything: rejected as stronger than current evidence.

Roadmap and cross references

Revisit after field-level schema mapping and end-to-end endpoint traces. See Controller runtime, Engine catalog, and AI readiness.
